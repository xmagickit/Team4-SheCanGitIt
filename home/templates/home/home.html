Women in Tech History

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace, the first computer programmer, wrote an algorithm for Charles Babbage’s Analytical Engine in the 1840s. Her work laid the foundation for modern computing.

Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper was a U.S. Navy rear admiral and computer scientist who developed the first compiler for a programming language. She helped create COBOL, one of the earliest high-level languages.

Margaret Hamilton

Margaret Hamilton was the lead software engineer for NASA’s Apollo program. She developed the onboard flight software that helped astronauts safely land on the Moon in 1969.

Katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was a trailblazing NASA mathematician whose calculations were critical for early U.S. spaceflights, including John Glenn’s orbital mission and the Apollo 11 Moon landing.
